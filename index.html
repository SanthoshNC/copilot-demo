Biography

A R Rahman was born in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India on 6th January 1967. He is an Indian composer, singer, and music producer. He is one of the most successful musicians in the Indian film industry. He has won several awards including two Academy Awards, two Grammy Awards, a BAFTA Award, a Golden Globe, four National Film Awards, fifteen Filmfare Awards, and thirteen Filmfare Awards South. He has also been awarded the Padma Bhushan, the third highest civilian award, in 2010 by the Government of India.

Rahman's film career began in 1992 when he started Panchathan Record Inn, a recording and mixing studio in his backyard. He initially composed music for documentaries and jingles for advertisements and Indian television channels. He later ventured into film music by composing the score and songs for the Tamil film Roja. He was also the music director for the Hindi film Rangeela.

Rahman has also been involved in several projects including the musical Bombay Dreams and the stage production of The Lord of the Rings. He has also been involved in several international projects including the Chinese film Warriors of Heaven and Earth and the Hollywood films Elizabeth: The Golden Age and 127 Hours.

Rahman has also been involved in several charitable causes including the Save the Children foundation. He has also been involved in several environmental causes including the Save the Tiger campaign.
